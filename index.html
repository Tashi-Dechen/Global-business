Global Business

It includes international trade(export & import) multinational companies and forign investment

Access to a global consumer base increases potential sales and profits.

Products, marketing, and management styles often need adjustment for local tastes.

Affects local economies through investment (like the $5.7 trillion international investment in the U.S. in 2024) and employment, but also creates complex tax and repatriation issues.

is a broad and dynamic topic that covers the activities, strategies, and challenges involved when companies expand their operations across national borders.

It's all about understanding how businesses operate, compete, and grow on a global scale.

Key Aspects

Operations: Unlike international businesses that only sell abroad, global firms have physical operations (factories, centers) worldwide.

Market Reach: Expands from local/national to a worldwide consumer base, increasing potential revenue.

Challenges:Navigating varied political climates, economic shifts, cultural nuances, regulations (like SPS/TBT measures), and currency exchange risks.

Strategic Focus: Involves global strategies, market entry, and adapting products/services to local tastes and systems

Globalization: The increasing interconnectedness of economies, cultures, and markets. It enables companies to operate internationally and consumers to access products from around the world.

Cultural differences: An important aspect of global business. Businesses must understand languages, traditions, values, and social norms to operate successfully in different countries.

International laws and regulations: Guide how businesses operate across borders. Companies must follow trade laws, labor laws, environmental rules, and tax systems of different countries.

Why it Matters

Drive Innovation: Global investment fuels growth, as seen with international companies investing in the U.S. economy.

Requires Special Skills:Success needs understanding of diverse contexts, risks, and opportunities.

Integral to Modern Economy: Most businesses, regardless of size, are impacted by the global marketplace.
