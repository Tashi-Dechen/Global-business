Basachu

Hydropower Project is a hydropower plant located on the Basochhu River in Wangdue Phodrang District of Bhutan.

he Basochhu River is a tributary of the Punatsangchhu River. The project is a run-of-the-river type, which means it generates electricity using the natural flow of the river without creating a large storage dam.

The project was developed in two phases. Phase I, with a capacity of 24 megawatts, was commissioned in 2001.

The project is owned and operated by Druk Green Power Corporation.

Basochhu Hydropower Project was developed with financial and technical support from the Government of Austria.

It is one of the earliest hydropower projects in Bhutan that involved European cooperation.

Its design helps minimize environmental impact, which aligns with Bhutan's policy of environmental conservation and sustainable development.
